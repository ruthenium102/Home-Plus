2. Information we collect

We collect only what the Service needs to run a shared family organiser:

CategoryExamplesWhy
Account The account holder’s email address and password (stored only as a secure hash). To create and secure your household account and sign you in.
Family profiles Each member’s display name, role (parent/child), colour, optional birthday, optional photo, and a profile PIN (stored only as a one-way hash). To show who’s who and to PIN-protect individual profiles on a shared device.
Household content Calendar events, chores, to-do lists, habits, rewards/points, meal plans and recipes, status/location labels (e.g. “At school”), and virtual-pet state. This is the content you create to organise your family. It is the core function of the app.

Approximate location (optional) Coarse device location, only if you enable the weather feature. To show local weather. Not stored on our servers; used at request time only.
Technical Basic app/device information needed to deliver and secure the Service. Security, reliability, and debugging.

We do not use advertising, we do not track you across other apps or websites, and we do not sell or rent personal information.

3. Children’s data

Home Plus is intended to be set up and managed by a parent or legal guardian. Child profiles are created by the account holder, who confirms at sign-up that they are 18 or older and the parent/guardian of the household, and who provides consent when adding each child profile.

  • Voice commands are off by default for child profiles. A parent must explicitly turn them on per child before any child voice transcript is processed.
  • We collect the minimum data needed to run the family organiser for a child profile (name, optional birthday, and the household content above).
  • A parent can review, edit, or delete a child’s profile and data at any time in the app, or delete the entire account (Section 8).
  • We do not knowingly use children’s data for any purpose other than providing the Service, and we do not show advertising or profile children.

4. How we use information

  • To provide, maintain, and secure the Service and sync it across your household’s devices.
  • To process voice commands you choose to send (Section 5).
  • To send essential service emails, such as family invitations and password resets.
  • To comply with our legal obligations and to detect and prevent abuse.

For users in the UK/EU, our legal bases are: performance of our contract with you (providing the Service), your consent (e.g. optional voice and weather), and our legitimate interests in keeping the Service secure and reliable.

5. Voice commands and AI

Voice commands are optional. When used, a short transcript of your request is sent to our AI sub-processor, Anthropic, solely to interpret the request (for example, to create a calendar event or list item). For child profiles this only happens after a parent has enabled voice for that child. 7. Data retention

We keep your household’s data for as long as your account is active. Optional location data for weather is used at request time and is not retained on our servers. Voice transcripts are processed to fulfil the command and are not used to build advertising profiles. When you delete your account, we delete your data as described in Section 8.

8. Your choices and rights

  • Access and edit: view and edit your family’s profiles and content in the app.
  • Delete your account: use Settings → Danger zone → Delete account to permanently delete your household account and associated data.
  • Optional features: voice and weather are off unless you turn them on, and can be turned off again at any time.
  • Depending on where you live, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, port, or restrict the processing of your personal data, and to lodge a complaint with your data protection authority. Contact us at [PRIVACY CONTACT EMAIL].

9. Security

Passwords and profile PINs are stored only as one-way hashes, never in plain text. Access to household data is restricted to members of that household through database-level security rules. No system is perfectly secure, but we work to protect your information.
